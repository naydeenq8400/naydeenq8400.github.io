Going To Dunkin

Me and my partner were assigned the task of creating a movie webpage project using our imagination, as well as all of our knowledge of everything we've learned this unit. Our movie is called "Going To Dunkin," and its abouyt the challenging decisions a teenager has to make when invited to go to Dunkin with their friends. We had a list of many idras we could've chose from, but this was the one we connected with the most.

We had to create a webpage suing all our knowledge learned from using a newly introduced webste, cs50.dev, to make our webpage, and add that to Github. We had to get ther hang of using many new commands, like git add ., git commit -m"message", git push, git pull, etc. We also had to insert a link to both of our portfolios, as well as our original planning page that helped the project run much more smoothly. Lastly, we added an image of our planning page to the README.md file.

The bigest challenge I had gone through was creating the planning page for the project. I was introduced to the use of Google Drawings for the first time, and had to tinker with that before being able to make my planning page. After much debate and negotiation, we were able to agree on a couple of ideas from each of us. Another challenge I had was collaborating outside of school, specifically when my partner or I weren't able to immediently respond to each others messages. We faced many issues when one of us would forget to pull changes, or forget to commit changes of our own. Fortunately, we were able to work through these issues. After making all these mistakes, we realized despite these mistakes and challenges, we were also improving our skills with every mistake made. Now, we've gained lots of knowledge from all this, as well as knowing which mistakes not to make.
